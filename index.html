Project Name:

Home Inventory Management System v1.0

Purpose:

The purpose of this project is to help users maintain a detailed inventory of their possessions, including prices and images of receipts. This system is particularly useful in the event of a fire or other disaster, as it provides proof of ownership for insurance purposes, thereby simplifying the claims process and ensuring that users can recover their losses more efficiently.

Goal:

To provide a secure and organized platform where users can store information about their belongings.

Objective 1:

Develop a secure authentication and authorization system using JSON Web Tokens (JWT) for user signup and login functionality.

Objective 2:

Implement a table structure for possessions with categorized data fields (category, name, year, serial number, photo URL, dollar value) to meet Minimum Viable Product (MVP) requirements.

Objective 3:

Design an intuitive and straightforward user interface for adding, editing, and managing inventory items.

Objective 4:

Ensure reliability and performance by creating a stable application that performs basic tasks without critical errors or crashes.

Objective 5:

Adhere to a cost-effective approach, considering budget constraints and avoiding excessive investment in non-essential features.

Objective 6:

Demonstrate the viability of the product as a proof of concept, showcasing its potential to solve the problem of managing home inventories effectively.

Frontend:

React.js, custom styling (CSS)

Backend:

Node.js, Express.js

Database:

PostgreSQL

Version Control:

Git, GitHub

Deployment:

Heroku

Contribution 1:

Built the server with user registration and authentication, including JWT for session management and encryption of sensitive information.

Contribution 2:

Developed the site infrastructure, created the React app boilerplate, and connected the frontend to the backend.

Contribution 3:

Deployed both the client and server to Heroku, ensuring the application was accessible and functioning correctly in a production environment.

Challenge 1: Tight Deadline

Our team faced the challenge of completing the entire web application within just five days, which required effective time management and prioritization of tasks.

  • Solution 1: We conducted a detailed project planning session at the outset, breaking down tasks into manageable segments and assigning priorities to ensure critical functionalities were developed first.
  • Solution 2: Daily stand-up meetings were held to track progress, identify bottlenecks early, and make necessary adjustments to meet the deadline.
  • Solution 3: Leveraging existing libraries, frameworks, and tools helped speed up development and reduce time spent on reinventing the wheel.
Challenge 2: Unforeseen Team Member Absence

One team member faced unexpected circumstances that required them to be absent for the first two days of the project, leading to a temporary gap in contribution and task allocation.

  • Solution 1: Responsibilities were redistributed among team members to maximize efficiency and ensure all project aspects were addressed effectively.
  • Solution 2: Regular updates and discussions were held to keep everyone informed and aligned with project goals and timelines.
  • Solution 3: Flexibility and adaptability within the team allowed us to address the temporary setback without compromising the overall progress of the project. I also took on a leadership role by proactively stepping in to cover additional tasks during the absence of a team member, while also providing support to another team member who required assistance with certain tasks. This approach fostered a collaborative and supportive environment within the team, ensuring that everyone felt empowered to contribute effectively.
